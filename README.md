# 🧠 Mind-to-Mind Communication

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Telepathy** | Direct mind-to-mind communication without using sensory channels. Practitioners claim to transmit thoughts, feelings, or images between individuals through focused intention and mental connection. |
| **Emotional telepathy** | The ability to sense or transmit emotional states between people without verbal or physical cues. Often described as feeling what another person is feeling across distances. |
| **Mental telepathy** | Transmission of specific thoughts, ideas, or mental images from one mind to another. Claims include both sending and receiving complex mental content. |
| **Dream telepathy** | Communication between minds during sleep states, where one person can allegedly send messages or images to another person's dreams or receive dream content from others. |
| **Animal telepathy** | Claimed ability to communicate mentally with animals, understanding their thoughts, needs, or intentions without verbal or behavioral cues. |
| **Group telepathy** | Shared mental communication among multiple people simultaneously, where thoughts or information flow through a connected group consciousness. |
| **Thought projection** | The practice of sending specific thoughts or mental images to another person through focused intention, often used in attempts at remote influence. |
| **Thought implantation** | The alleged ability to place specific thoughts or ideas into another person's mind without their conscious awareness, often discussed in mind control contexts. |
| **Silent communication** | Nonverbal mental exchange of information between individuals, relying on perceived mental connection rather than spoken or written language. |
| **Mind linking** | Creating a temporary or permanent mental connection between two or more minds, allowing for direct sharing of thoughts, sensations, or experiences. |
| **Consciousness linking** | A deeper form of mind connection where individuals share not just thoughts but entire states of consciousness or awareness. |
| **Hive mind** | A collective consciousness where individual minds merge into a unified group intelligence, with shared thoughts and diminished individual identity. |
| **Collective consciousness** | The shared beliefs, ideas, and moral attitudes that operate as a unifying force within society, or in paranormal contexts, a literal shared mental field. |
| **Shared consciousness** | Multiple individuals experiencing the same conscious state simultaneously, often through meditation, ritual, or claimed psychic connection. |
| **Universal consciousness** | The concept that all minds are connected to or part of a single, all-encompassing consciousness that contains all thoughts and experiences. |
| **Mind synchronization** | The process of aligning brainwave patterns or mental states between individuals, often through meditation, entrainment, or focused attention. |
| **Brain entrainment** | Using rhythmic stimuli (sound, light) to synchronize brainwave frequencies between people or to specific states, potentially facilitating mental connection. |
| **Brain-to-brain interface (technology)** | Direct electronic communication between brains using sensors and stimulators, allowing transmission of neural signals without speech or gesture. |

# 👁️ Eye-Based Concepts

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Hypnotic gaze** | A focused, intense stare used in hypnosis to induce trance states or suggestibility in subjects. The practitioner maintains unwavering eye contact to capture attention and bypass critical thinking. |
| **Mesmeric gaze** | Similar to hypnotic gaze, derived from Franz Mesmer's animal magnetism theory. Uses eye contact to allegedly channel magnetic energy and induce altered states or healing. |
| **Psychic gaze** | The claimed ability to transmit thoughts, influence, or read minds through eye contact alone. Practitioners believe eyes are windows to consciousness that can send or receive psychic information. |
| **Eye telepathy** | A specific form of telepathy where communication occurs primarily through eye contact, with thoughts allegedly transmitted directly through visual connection. |
| **Eye projection** | The practice of projecting mental energy, intentions, or influence through the eyes toward another person or object, often used in attempts at remote influence. |
| **Ocular influence** | The broader concept of affecting others' thoughts, emotions, or behaviors through eye contact, gaze patterns, or focused visual attention. |
| **Silent gaze communication** | Nonverbal communication through prolonged eye contact, where meaning or information is allegedly exchanged without words through the eyes alone. |
| **Evil eye** (belief found in many cultures) | A widespread belief that certain people can cause harm, misfortune, or illness through a malevolent stare or envious glance. Found in Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and other cultures. |
| **Fascination gaze** | An intense, captivating stare used to hold someone's attention and induce suggestibility, often employed in hypnosis, persuasion, or romantic contexts. |
| **Magnetic gaze** | A powerful, confident eye contact technique claimed to attract, influence, or mesmerize others. Used in charisma training, hypnosis, and social influence practices. |
| **Stare induction** | Using prolonged staring or fixed gaze to induce trance states, altered consciousness, or suggestibility in oneself or others. |

# 🌀 Hypnosis & Suggestibility

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Hypnosis** | A trance state characterized by focused attention, increased suggestibility, and deep relaxation. Used therapeutically for behavior modification, pain management, and accessing subconscious material. |
| **Self-hypnosis** | The practice of inducing hypnotic states in oneself through techniques like progressive relaxation, visualization, or auto-suggestion. Used for self-improvement, stress reduction, and personal change. |
| **Conversational hypnosis** | Also known as covert hypnosis, using language patterns, storytelling, and indirect suggestion to influence others during normal conversation without formal trance induction. |
| **Rapid hypnosis** | Techniques to induce hypnotic trance quickly, often in seconds or minutes, using shock, confusion, or pattern interrupts to bypass critical thinking rapidly. |
| **Deep hypnosis** | A profound trance state where the subject experiences deep relaxation, time distortion, and high suggestibility. Used for therapeutic work and accessing deep subconscious material. |
| **Ericksonian hypnosis** | Developed by Milton Erickson, using permissive language, metaphors, storytelling, and indirect suggestion to work with the subconscious mind in a natural, conversational style. |
| **Stage hypnosis** | Entertainment hypnosis performed for audiences, using suggestibility tests and group dynamics to create dramatic demonstrations of trance phenomena and suggestibility. |
| **Regression hypnosis** | Using hypnosis to guide subjects back to earlier life experiences, often childhood memories or past life memories, to explore psychological patterns or heal trauma. |
| **Post-hypnotic suggestion** | Suggestions given during hypnosis that are carried out after the subject awakens, often triggered by specific cues or situations. Used for behavior change and therapeutic goals. |
| **Suggestibility** | The degree to which a person responds to suggestions, whether in hypnosis or everyday life. Varies among individuals and can be measured through suggestibility tests. |
| **Trance induction** | The process of guiding someone into a hypnotic trance using techniques like progressive relaxation, eye fixation, confusion, or visualization to alter consciousness. |
| **Mesmerism** | An 18th-century theory by Franz Mesmer proposing that an invisible fluid (animal magnetism) could be channeled to induce healing and trance states. Precursor to modern hypnosis. |
| **Animal magnetism** | Mesmer's term for the supposed universal fluid that flows between all living things. Practitioners believed they could manipulate this fluid to heal or induce trance through passes and gaze. |

# 🔮 Psychic Information Gathering

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Clairvoyance** (seeing) | The alleged ability to gain information about an object, person, location, or event through extrasensory perception. Practitioners claim to see visions, images, or symbols that reveal hidden information. |
| **Clairaudience** (hearing) | The claimed ability to perceive sounds or voices from the spiritual realm or receive auditory information without physical ears. Often described as hearing messages from guides or spirits. |
| **Clairsentience** (feeling) | The ability to sense or feel information through physical or emotional sensations. Practitioners report feeling others' emotions, physical sensations, or environmental information through their body. |
| **Claircognizance** (knowing) | The phenomenon of suddenly knowing something without having learned it through normal means. Described as intuitive knowing or downloading information that just appears in consciousness. |
| **Clairgustance** (taste) | The claimed ability to taste substances without physical contact, often used in psychic readings to gain information about people or situations through the sense of taste. |
| **Clairalience** (smell) | The ability to smell scents that have no physical source, often associated with spiritual presence or gaining information about people or places through olfactory impressions. |
| **Remote viewing** | A protocolized practice of perceiving distant or unseen targets using extrasensory perception. Developed for military/intelligence applications, it uses structured methods to describe locations, objects, or events. |
| **Remote sensing** | The broader ability to gather information about distant targets through psychic means, similar to remote viewing but sometimes less structured in methodology. |
| **Psychometry** | The claimed ability to read information about people or events by touching physical objects. Practitioners believe objects retain energy or memories that can be accessed through touch. |
| **Precognition** | The alleged ability to perceive or predict future events before they occur through dreams, visions, or intuitive knowing. Also called premonition or future sight. |
| **Retrocognition** | The ability to perceive past events or access information about the past through extrasensory means, often described as seeing or knowing about historical events without having learned them. |
| **Presentiment** | The phenomenon of having a feeling or physiological response before an event occurs, often measured in parapsychology studies as anticipating random stimuli before they happen. |
| **Intuition** (ordinary and extraordinary claims) | Ordinary intuition refers to unconscious pattern recognition and gut feelings based on experience. Extraordinary claims suggest intuition can access information beyond normal sensory input. |
| **Extrasensory perception (ESP)** | The general term for perceiving information through means other than the known senses, including telepathy, clairvoyance, precognition, and other psychic phenomena. |

# ⚡ Mind Affecting Matter

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Psychokinesis (PK)** | The alleged ability of the mind to influence matter, energy, or physical systems without physical interaction. Includes various subcategories like telekinesis and other "kinesis" abilities. |
| **Telekinesis** | The claimed ability to move or manipulate objects with the mind alone, without physical contact. Often demonstrated in fiction but lacks scientific evidence in reality. |
| **Biokinesis** | The supposed ability to manipulate biological processes, including one's own body or others', through mental intention. Claims include changing eye color, healing, or altering DNA. |
| **Cryokinesis** | The fictional ability to manipulate ice, cold, and temperature through mental power. Popular in fantasy and superhero media but not scientifically supported. |
| **Pyrokinesis** | The claimed ability to create, control, or manipulate fire with the mind. Featured in fiction but has no scientific basis; actual fire manipulation requires physical means. |
| **Electrokinesis** | The supposed ability to generate, control, or manipulate electricity and electrical phenomena through mental power alone. Fictional concept without scientific evidence. |
| **Hydrokinesis** | The alleged ability to manipulate water and liquid through mental intention. A common theme in fiction but not supported by scientific research. |
| **Aerokinesis** | The claimed ability to control air, wind, and atmospheric conditions through the mind. Fictional concept found in fantasy stories but not demonstrated in reality. |
| **Geokinesis** | The supposed ability to manipulate earth, rock, soil, and geological features through mental power. Fictional ability without scientific evidence. |
| **Chronokinesis** | The fictional ability to manipulate time, including slowing, speeding, stopping, or reversing time through mental power. Exists only in science fiction and fantasy. |
| **Vitakinesis** | The claimed ability to manipulate life force, healing, or biological vitality through mental intention. Combines concepts of energy healing with fictional powers. |
| **Healing intention** | The practice of directing focused mental intention toward healing others or oneself. Studied in alternative medicine and some parapsychology research for potential effects on health. |
| **Mind over matter** | The general concept that mental states, beliefs, or intentions can influence physical reality. Ranges from placebo effects to claims of direct matter manipulation. |

> **Note:** Most of the "kinesis" concepts above are fictional or paranormal claims rather than scientifically established phenomena.

# 🧪 Parapsychology Terms

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Psi** | The general term used in parapsychology for psychic phenomena or processes, encompassing both ESP (extrasensory perception) and PK (psychokinesis). |
| **ESP** | Extrasensory perception, the ability to acquire information without using the known senses. Includes telepathy, clairvoyance, precognition, and other psychic information gathering. |
| **PK** | Psychokinesis, the alleged ability of the mind to influence matter or physical systems without physical interaction. Also called mind-matter interaction. |
| **Ganzfeld experiments** | A parapsychology research method using mild sensory deprivation (uniform light and sound) to induce a state conducive to ESP, testing for telepathy or clairvoyance. |
| **Forced-choice ESP** | Experimental tests where subjects must choose from a limited set of options (like Zener cards) to demonstrate ESP, allowing statistical analysis of results. |
| **Free-response ESP** | Experimental tests where subjects provide open-ended descriptions or impressions rather than choosing from fixed options, used in remote viewing and similar protocols. |
| **Psi missing** | A phenomenon in parapsychology research where subjects score significantly below chance, suggesting unconscious avoidance or psychological factors affecting results. |
| **Psi-mediated interaction** | The concept that psi phenomena might operate through interaction between consciousness and reality rather than simple information transfer or direct influence. |
| **Anomalous cognition** | A neutral term for information acquisition that appears to occur without normal sensory input, used in parapsychology research to avoid loaded terms like ESP. |
| **Anomalous perturbation** | A neutral term for mind-matter interaction or influence on physical systems, used in parapsychology research to describe PK-like effects without assuming mechanisms. |

# 🤖 Modern Scientific & Technological Research

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Brain-computer interface (BCI)** | Technology that enables direct communication between the brain and external devices, translating neural signals into commands for computers, prosthetics, or other systems. |
| **Brain-to-brain interface (BBI)** | Experimental technology allowing direct communication between brains, typically using BCI systems to transmit neural signals from one brain to another via computer mediation. |
| **Neural decoding** | The process of interpreting neural signals to determine the information they represent, such as visual images, thoughts, or intended movements, using machine learning and pattern recognition. |
| **Neural stimulation** | Techniques that directly stimulate neural tissue using electrical, magnetic, or other methods to modulate brain activity, used in research, therapy, and brain-computer interfaces. |
| **EEG communication** | Using electroencephalography (EEG) to detect brain signals and translate them into communication or control signals, enabling people to communicate or control devices through brain activity. |
| **Neural synchronization** | The phenomenon of brainwaves or neural activity patterns aligning between individuals, studied in relation to social interaction, empathy, and potential brain-to-brain communication. |
| **Hyperscanning** | Simultaneous brain scanning of multiple people during social interaction, allowing researchers to study brain-to-brain coupling and neural synchronization during communication or cooperation. |
| **Neurofeedback** | A technique that provides real-time information about brain activity, allowing individuals to learn to self-regulate their brainwaves for therapeutic or performance enhancement purposes. |
| **Transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS)** | A non-invasive technique using magnetic fields to stimulate specific brain regions, used in research, depression treatment, and studying brain function. |
| **Transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS)** | A non-invasive brain stimulation technique using weak electrical currents to modulate neural activity, researched for cognitive enhancement and therapeutic applications. |

# 📊 Evidence Classification

## ✅ Strong scientific support

- Hypnosis
- Self-hypnosis
- Neurofeedback
- Brain-computer interfaces
- Brain-to-brain interfaces (simple experimental forms)
- Neural synchronization
- Emotional contagion
- Subliminal perception (limited effects)
- Mirror neuron-related research (within neuroscience)

## 🔬 Under active research or debated

- Presentiment
- Ganzfeld experiments
- Some parapsychology research into psi

## ❓ Speculative, spiritual, or lacking reliable scientific evidence

- Telepathy
- Eye telepathy
- Thought projection
- Remote viewing
- Clairvoyance
- Psychokinesis
- Astral projection (as literal travel)
- Morphic resonance
- Akashic records
- Tulpa as an independent external entity
- Egregores
- Bilocation
- Nonlocal consciousness (as a demonstrated phenomenon)
